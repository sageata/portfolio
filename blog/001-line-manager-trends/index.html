Rediscovering My Passion for Coding: A Journey from Line Manager to Data Analyst

Note that the data in the chart above is fictional

Although I graduated from an IT high school in 2001 with a focus on Junior Analysis and Programming, I didn’t fully immerse myself in the tech world until 2016. That year, I took on a role as a Line Manager and rediscovered my passion for coding.

The Challenge

As Line Managers, one of our responsibilities was to collect data on non-compliances observed during flights. This task was initially carried out using Excel spreadsheets, with each manager tracking their observations. At the end of each month, a few of us would manually compile these non-compliances from over 40 Excel files, a process that was not only time-consuming but also error-prone.

The Turning Point

Frustrated by the inefficiency and loss of critical data during this process, I knew there had to be a better way. With the support of my managers, Natasha Milne and Paul Starkey, I developed a simple coding system to streamline the data collection process. This system used specific codes to tag non-compliances directly in our reports, making it easier to categorize and analyze the data.

The Solution

After a brief training session for my fellow Line Managers, we began using the new system. I then employed Power BI and Power Query to automate the data extraction process. The result? Updating the monthly report became as easy as clicking ‘refresh’â€”all while enjoying a cup of coffee.

The Unexpected Bonus

What I hadn’t anticipated was how this project would make me proficient in Power BI, a tool that was relatively unknown in 2016 but has since become a gold standard in corporate data analysis.

Key Outcomes

  • Efficiency Boost: Reduced monthly work hours from 160-200 to just 30 minutes.
  • Process Improvement: Identified that 80% of non-compliances occurred during briefing and pre-boarding, leading to simplified key processes.
  • Data Insights: Uncovered observer bias as a significant factor in non-compliance reporting, leading to more targeted training.
  • Stakeholder Engagement: Created visual reports that could be easily shared with key stakeholders, enhancing transparency and accountability.
